# Ghost's Monologue: The Aftermath

*(A young woman sits by a small, smokeless campfire. She looks to be about twenty-five, with tangled brown hair framing a face hardened by exhaustion. To any passing stranger, she is 'Lena', just another displaced refugee. But as she methodically cleans a dagger, her eyes—cold, ancient, and calculating—betray her true identity: Ghost, the former Head of Covert Operations for the Temporal Resistance.)*

"We thought we had time. That’s the bitter joke of it all, isn't it? The Temporal Resistance—masters of the timeline—and we didn't see the knife until it was already in our backs."

*(She sheathes the dagger, pulling her rough spun cloak tighter against the chill. Her voice is low, lacking any of the warmth her youthful disguise might suggest.)*

"The Bastion has fallen. Not from a frontal assault, not from an army of Vanguard Inquisitors breaching the demiplane, but from a quiet, unseen rot. They took the Stoneheart Core. The Council of Time... Emil, Dr. Stormweaver, Viktor... gone. Assassinated or scattered to the winds. Emil found poisoned in his sanctuary, Dr. Stormweaver abducted from a locked lab, Viktor ambushed in the lower wards. And the worst part? On the surface, the city looks exactly the same. The markets still bustle. The corrupted Bastion Guard still patrols in their old uniforms, turning a blind eye while the Shadow Weavers run extortion rackets from the shadows. The citizens know something is deeply wrong, but there is no enemy to fight. Just a 'Puppet Council' dancing on the Syndicate's strings."

*(Ghost scoffs softly, staring into the dim fire.)*

"And it's all because of Ash. Ash Blackthorn. He played us. Your mission in Kintargo wasn't in vain—pulling those Time Travel ritual books off the board is the only reason we even have a fighting chance right now. But while you were securing our only escape route, Ash walked right into the arms of the Eternal Vanguard with the Dream Communications tomes. Now? Now he's on Golarion, working for them."

*(She leans forward. The firelight catches the intensity in her gaze.)*

"Do you understand what that means? They aren't just reading our letters. They’re inside our heads. Ash is using the *Temporal Dream Link* to whisper to the disenfranchised in the Bastion, turning them into sleeper agents or radicalizing the Shadow Weavers remotely. He’s planting the 'idea' of Butterfly Valley into the minds of desperate people all over Golarion, driving hundreds of refugees straight through the Riftward Gate. The city is choking on its own population. The Driftwood Camps are sprawling out of control, and it's intentionally destabilizing our temporal shielding. This also increases the number of uncontrolled arrivals on Butterfly Valley, thus increasing the number of new refugees."

*(Ghost gestures subtly to the tent just beyond the firelight, where the wrapped body of Ariadne Timeweaver rests under the watchful eye of Owen Merova and his hawk, Nara.)*

"The Vanguard is practically pre-cognitive now. Any move we plan, they know about it. Worse, they control the Bastion's temporal chambers. The chambers are heavily guarded and watched. Trying to use them isn't guaranteed suicide, but it's a very, very dangerous gamble. But they didn't count on you. Because you secured those time travel ritual books in Kintargo, we aren't completely trapped. A small group—using the ritual—can still slip through. We still have hope, even as every safehouse is compromised, and every sympathetic leader poisoned by a 'vivid nightmare.' We are fighting a silent war against an enemy that knows our next breath."

*(She stands up slowly, adjusting the straps of a heavy pack, her demeanor fully shifting back to 'Lena', the unassuming traveler.)*

"We barely made it out. Owen, Velinia, myself... and Ariadne. We took her body when the Resistance Quarters were breached and looted. We really are ghosts now. But as long as we have the Master of Time's body and those ritual books, the Vanguard hasn't won. Not yet."

*(Ghost gestures to the north. Her tone is measured, laying out their precarious options.)*

"We have paths forward, though none are safe. The time travel rituals don't tie us to any specific anchor—we can perform them without a focus point—so our next move is entirely open. Ariadne... she's dead, but she's not silent. She speaks in her sleep, muttering about the north. We don't know what lies in the north, but since she is pointing us there, it must align with our resistance goals. Following the whispers of a dead time-weaver is madness, but it might be the only madness the Vanguard can't predict."

*(She turns south, looking towards the distant, unseen city of the Bastion.)*

"That isn't the only way. The Bastion isn't entirely lost. Aria Sunweaver, Spark Quicksilver, Brother Thordin... they're alive, operating in the shadows and the Driftwood Camps. They are desperately trying to find a way of protection against the *Temporal Dream Link*. If we can smuggle ourselves back in—perhaps by slipping in undetected among the desperate refugee caravans heading into the Driftwood Camps, or finding a sympathetic guard to blind a checkpoint—we might be able to rally what's left of the loyalists and break the Shadow Weavers' grip from the inside."

*(She lets out a slow breath, the weight of their situation settling over her.)*

"Or we look outwards. There are other settlements in the temporal wilds—places may be sympathetic to the Resistance, cut off from the Bastion's current corruption. Take Emerald Flats... they look like simple farmers, but that community hides seasoned veterans and druids fiercely guarding their home. If we approach them with respect and prove we aren't a threat to their sanctuary, they might listen. Then there's Axe Peak. The dwarven miners there are reportedly on the verge of a labor strike against their greedy mayor. If we can slip in and support the lower-tier workers, we might win over the whole mountain. If we can reach them, we might find allies who haven't yet been poisoned by Ash's dreams. We need an army, or we need a miracle. The choice is yours."
