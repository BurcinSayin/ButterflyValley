## HEADLINES

### STARWATCH STRUGGLES AT ANTLER LODGE
**Eastgate** — In the wake of yesterday's horrific display of fleshy magical anomalies at the Antler Lodge, the Starwatch finds itself stretched thin. Second Commander Alasmin Sarulamon has deployed additional patrols to Eastgate, but the search for the missing children has yielded little fruit. Erastilian clerics are demanding protection from further Pharasmin inquisitions, while Starwatch officials privately grumble about Captain Asilia's continued focus on the *Hurricane Wings* while the city's streets face magical crises.

### PREPARATIONS FOR LORD ABSOL'S 400TH STALL
**Petal District** — The grand parade planned for the 400th birthday of venerable dwarven architect Lord Absol Tullman IV has hit an unexpected snag. The Temple of Torag's parade committee has clashed with Petal District beautification officials over the weight of the ceremonial stone floats. "We're not ruining the cobblestones just because a dwarf refused to die on schedule," quipped one district clerk. Lord Absol himself could not be reached for comment, as he was reportedly napping at Magpie Manor.

### AARNOCK'S "AUTUMN VEIL" SELLS OUT IN HOURS
**Ivy District** — Grand Alchemist Aarnock Xanthiss of the Perfumers' Conglomerate has done it again. His latest fragrance, "Autumn Veil," sold out within three hours of its release this morning. Aristocrats are already bidding triple the retail price on the secondary market. Xanthiss was seen smiling broadly outside his Ivy District shop, assuring customers that a second batch would be brewed in time for next week's galas. 

### NAVAR'S NEW NOVEL DEEMED "TOO DOUR" FOR DELICATE SENSIBILITIES
**Ivy District** — Reclusive author Aethlred Navar's latest manuscript, *The Attic's Promise*, has leaked to a few select members of the Bloom Cabaret, and the reviews are polarizing. While his usual fans praise the bleak, fatalistic prose, several prominent socialites have complained that the tale of an ancestral curse is "excessively grim" for the current social season. Navar has reportedly locked himself in his thirteenth writing parlor and refuses to speak to his publisher.

### MYSTERY BUYER SWEEPS IVY DISTRICT GALLERY
**Ivy District** — Following the abrupt cancellation of painter Valaen's exhibition at the White Grotto, rumors are swirling about the true fate of the forty apocalyptic canvases. While censors claim the closure was a matter of public decency, Tribune sources suggest a single, wealthy buyer acquired the entire collection before the doors even opened. Aspiring artists are now frantically trying to replicate Valaen's doomsday scenes, hoping to catch the eye of this mysterious patron.
