## 🗞️ CITYWIDE HEADLINES

### TERRIFYING MAGICS AND CHAOS AT THE ANTLER LODGE!
**Eastgate** — Chaos erupted early yesterday at the **Antler Lodge**, a well-known temple of Erastil currently serving as a convalescent ward for victims of the ongoing "children's fever." Witnesses report that shortly after a visit from Pharasmin High Priest Morvan, a horrifying display of dark magic—a grotesque wall of living flesh and writhing tentacles—suddenly blocked the ward's corridors.

Panic spilled into the streets when a young girl’s voice was heard screaming that temple guards were "murdering children." While temple authorities and House Blakros sponsors have downplayed the incident, the Starwatch is currently investigating the bizarre disturbance and the whereabouts of several missing patients.

Could this mean holy war in the convalescent wards? Bystanders report that just after the terrifying magical display, a furious shouting match echoed from the courtyard between devotees of Erastil and the visiting **Pharasmin High Priest Morvan**. Morvan allegedly accused the Erastilians of "harboring unnatural abominations," to which "Eastgate's grandmother" Zepah reportedly threatened to feed the inquisitor to her own prize boars! With Starwatch **Captain Asilia of Gyr** personally stepping in to keep the quarrel between the two clerics apart, citizens are now taking bets: was the tentacle horror a Pharasmin curse meant to purge the ward, or a desperate Erastilian defense against Morvan's zealous purge?

Adding fuel to the fire, the Starwatch has quietly dispatched specialized patrols across Eastgate to hunt down several missing children who escaped the Antler Lodge during the chaos. Gossip in the local taverns suggests these weren't just terrified patients fleeing the tentacles. One terrified merchant claims he saw a pack of fever-addled youths effortlessly scale a sheer brick wall near the Blue Tower, while another swears she saw a runaway child with eyes that glowed like cold stars. Are these simply sick orphans lost in the city, or is the mysterious "children's fever" transforming them into something the House Blakros sponsors want kept secret at any cost?

### BRIEF PANIC INSIDE THE NEW THASSILONIAN EMBASSY
**Foreign Quarter** — A sudden, brief panic swept through the public halls of the **New Thassilonian Embassy** yesterday afternoon. According to nervous eyewitnesses visiting the consulate's public reception area, a momentary but intense magical anomaly triggered the internal wards, causing guards to draw weapons and swiftly secure the corridors. Eyewitnesses claim the chaos was sparked when a strange, billowing smoke filled the room and a young child seemingly transformed into a grown adult woman before vanishing into the private sections of the embassy.

The disturbance was contained before any sign of it reached the street outside, and Lord Firandivar has officially declined to comment, citing internal diplomatic affairs. However, the swift lockdown has fueled ongoing speculation regarding the secretive nature of the Thassilonian mission here in the city.

Speaking of the Thassilonian Embassy, eagle-eyed night owls report that none other than the Irorium’s darling champion, **Atrandi Goldheart**, has been spotted slipping away from the embassy grounds at truly scandalous hours. Has the popular gladiator found a wealthy new patron among the diplomats, or is a forbidden romance blooming behind those heavily warded gates? Some arena gossips are spinning a far wilder tale: that the gladiator is caught in a scandalous love triangle with a Thassilonian official and the mysterious, fully-grown woman who materialized from the smoke! Bookies are already taking bets on whether the embassy's recent "internal lockdown" was merely a smokescreen for a magical lovers' quarrel!

## 🌆 DAILY DISPATCHES

### CLINICS OVERFLOW IN THE PUDDLES
**The Puddles** — City physicians issued an emergency advisory at dawn regarding a severe fever spreading rapidly through the crowded slums of The Puddles. Concurrently, the mysterious "children's disease" continues to puzzle clerics across the Inner Sea. Charitable wards and Primarch's-name almshouses are currently operating at maximum capacity.

Is the fever truly to blame for the overflowing clinics? Some locals whisper that the sudden surge in patients is actually the result of a vicious, underground turf war that the Muckrakers are desperately trying to conceal. Others claim the city's alchemists are quietly using the crowded wards to test volatile, unproven "cures" for the children's disease on the destitute. Whatever the truth, unbadged physicians have reportedly been seen loading the worst afflicted into unmarked carriages under the cover of night!

### SCANDAL ROCKS THE IVY DISTRICT EXHIBITION
**Ivy District** — Tonight's highly anticipated gallery opening by celebrated painter Valaen has been abruptly canceled by district censors. Sources claim Valaen has obsessively painted the exact same mysterious, apocalyptic catastrophe in over forty canvases and refuses to say who commissioned them or what event they depict. Local artists are planning a protest outside the White Grotto this evening.

High society whispers suggest the censors didn't shut down Valaen's gallery—**High Hetaera Dyrianna** did. Rumor has it the Calistrian spymaster personally bought all forty of the apocalyptic canvases for an exorbitant sum, having recognized a deeply guarded secret painted into the ruins. Now, every aspiring artist in the district is desperately trying to paint their own catastrophe in hopes of attracting House Avenstar's lucrative, and dangerous, patronage!

### COUNTERFEIT CHAOS GRIPS THE COINS
**The Coins** — A fierce "wrong-mint" counterfeit scandal paralyzed morning trade in the Grand Bazaar today. Half the moneychangers in The Coins are currently refusing silver from a rival banking house, accusing them of guild-war sabotage. Shoppers are advised to carry gold or rely on established letters of credit until the Grand Council resolves the dispute.

Who really minted the "wrong" silver? Some merchants insist the rival banking house is just a front for **Scion Lord Avid**, who is using Qadiran gold to quietly buy up the district's debts and squeeze out the old moneychangers. Others claim the counterfeit silver isn't fake at all, but rather ancient Thassilonian coin dug up from the Precipice Quarter cellars and washed through the Bazaar. Either way, the Guild of Wonders' sanctioned assassins have reportedly been seen sharpening their blades around the banks!
