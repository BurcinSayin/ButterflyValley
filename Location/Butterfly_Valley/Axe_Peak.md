# Axe Peak: The Chopped Plateau

## Overview

Axe Peak is a mining company and settlement, populated mostly by dwarves, situated in the Butterfly Valley demiplane. Located approximately one and a half weeks of road travel to the northwest of Bastion City, it is the primary source of ores, minerals, and stone for the region's expanding infrastructure. The settlement's name originates from the distinct shape of the hill it occupies—the top of the peak looks exactly as though it were cleanly chopped off by a massive axe, leaving a large flat plateau.

## Geography

The main settlement rests at the top of the hill where the plateau is located. However, due to the high dwarven population, the community extends deep into the earth, making extensive use of underground caverns and structures beneath the plateau. In the surrounding region, there are multiple hills that are also used for mining, but the main peak remains the primary resource hub. The climate is noticeably colder than the valley floor, requiring sturdy architecture and resilient inhabitants.

## Economy and Mining

The lifeblood of Axe Peak is its extensive network of mines. The community extracts a variety of valuable resources:
- Iron and copper ore for tools and weapons
- Coal for heating and industry
- Precious gems and rare minerals found deep within the mountain
- High-quality building stone

The work is dangerous and physically demanding, but the rewards are substantial. Trade convoys regularly travel the perilous mountain roads to exchange these resources for food and supplies from Bastion City and Emerald Flats.

## Sustenance and Water

While trade provides much of their goods, the settlement actively supplements its food supply by cultivating extensive mushroom farms in the damp, darker caverns of the mountain. They also hunt the resilient wildlife that roams the rigorous mountain slopes and upper tunnel systems. For freshwater, the dwarves rely heavily on collecting as much rainwater as possible across the flat expanse of the chopped plateau. Fortunately, some of the deeper caverns within the peak also house natural, subterranean freshwater springs, ensuring a steady, albeit carefully managed, water supply.

## Culture and Society

The people of Axe Peak, mostly dwarves, are tough, pragmatic, and fiercely loyal to one another. The shared dangers of mining foster a strong sense of camaraderie and brotherhood. 

- **Tavern Culture:** After long shifts in the mines, the community gathers in the underground dwarven taverns to share stories, ale, and forge strong bonds.
- **Craftsmanship:** While raw materials are the main export, many inhabitants are also skilled blacksmiths and stonemasons, taking pride in their craft.
- **Superstitions:** Miners often adhere to various superstitions and rituals to ensure safety underground.

## Defense and Security

Due to the dangerous environment and valuable resources, Axe Peak is well-defended. The community boasts a strong, professional guard force consisting of experienced fighters and retired miners. The natural terrain provides excellent defensive advantages against potential threats.
